Democracy demands informed voters.

What is VotingInfo.ca?

I created this website to act as a gateway to sources of information potential voters would find useful in order to make informed decisions. The goal of the website is to provide a non-biased representation of all parties by publicly sourcing assistance in compiling the information from many external resources.

The website aims to meet and provide solutions to these problems voters face today:

  • Inform voters on candidate political representations on core questions posed by voters.
  • Inform politicians about public opinion and desires and how voters wish to see questions answered.
  • Provide links to party/organization sites, platform summaries, and public communications to enable informed voting.

When I went to perform my civic duty of voting I felt as though I was not getting the information I needed in order to make an informed decision on who I was electing and was picking my representative by party affiliation only. Many new voters may feel like they do not have definitive sources for information or ways they can compare their political views to potential candidates. This website aims to open the flood gates of communication between the politicians and their electorate which should in turn improve voter turn out and ensure correct public representation.

This website is not run by the government, or any political parties. The intended purpose of the website is to provide a fair and unbiased representation, to connect voters to the key information they need to learn more about the choices they will be making.
